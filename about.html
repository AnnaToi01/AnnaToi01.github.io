About

I grew up in Tbilisi (Georgia) with mostly two things on my mind: Biology and music. So I spent my days traveling to Biology Olympiads, or practicing the piano. At 17, I left Georgia to do my bachelor's degree in Germany, after that I spent a year in Okinawa (Japan) as a researcher, and did a master's degree in Zurich.

I'm slightly shy, but I enjoy spending time with new and old friends. I care about science and my research in evolutionary biology. I care about music: I love finding experimental music in the depth of the internet, I love going to concerts and I'm learning to play the organ. I like fashion, where I negotiate aesthetic influences from Georgia with contemporary ideas that excite me. And generally, I like science, I like things that are social and create a sense of belonging, and things that are creative and experimental.
